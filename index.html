What Else Is There To Do?

The internet has been shut down for a year. People are going crazy. No Tik Tok's, Instagram, Facebook . . . What else is there to do?

"Well go outside of course!" said a fortune teller for the elderly. "You could paint something, play the guitar, learn to play the guitar, write a book, take a nap, watch a good movie, eat some fries, learn how to make candles, drink some water, plant flowers, wash your hands, pet some kittens, befriend a sentient roll of toilet paper, play Candyland, befriend an insecure celery stalk who can’t accept that he tastes bad, adopt a guinea pig, make sure theres toilet paper before you go in to take a poo, foresight folks. . . . So yeah, anyways theres lots to do."

Remember your awesome childhood when playing outside was a thing. Climbing trees, riding bikes, riding scooters and getting hit by it repeatedly in the ankle daily, but it was worth it. Lifting up rocks and checking out the bugs underneath . . . those gross sand pits that were supposed to be fun but were basically just giant cat litter boxes for the neighborhood. 

Which one of these things would you like to do first? What would you like to learn? However, after this year, how your life is going to be? Stick to your “new lifestyle” for a year or go back to the internet life?

Gen Z are so used to the internet revolving around their lives, and when it gets stripped from right under you, you don’t know how to live. Future generations adapted to the new world and suddenly, the world became a better place. Every sustainable aspect flourished and it was happily ever after.
